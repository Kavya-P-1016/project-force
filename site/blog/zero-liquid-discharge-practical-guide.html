Zero liquid discharge: a practical guide before you commit capex

ZLD is powerful, but not always the first right step. The best projects start with a reuse hierarchy and a realistic concentrate management plan.

Start with water balance, not equipment brochures

Map every incoming and outgoing stream, then classify which uses demand high-quality water and which can tolerate lower-quality reclaimed streams.

Where ZLD is usually justified

  • High TDS discharge constraints with limited disposal routes.
  • Water-scarce zones where fresh water replacement is expensive.
  • Clusters with repeat non-compliance history and strict monitoring.

Implementation model that works

Most plants de-risk with phase-wise execution: improve primary and biological treatment first, then add concentration and crystallization modules.
