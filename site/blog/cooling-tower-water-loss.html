Reducing cooling tower water loss: proven tactics

Cooling towers silently consume more water than almost any other utility on a typical process site. The good news: most plants can cut make-up water 15–25% with three disciplined interventions — and none of them require ripping out the tower.

1. Fix drift first

Drift loss on an older tower can easily be 10× the spec value if the eliminators are damaged or missing. A weekend replacement of the drift eliminator pack is the single highest-ROI intervention on most sites.

2. Optimise Cycles of Concentration (COC)

Running the tower at a COC of 3 when the chemistry allows 6 means you are discharging twice as much water as you need. Proper chemical dosing, combined with real-time conductivity control on blowdown, is the classic 20% saving.

3. Side-stream filtration

Suspended solids in the circulating loop degrade heat transfer and force early blowdown. A 5–10% side-stream filter at the correct pore size routinely pays back in under two years just on water and chemical savings.

The instrumentation that makes it stick

  • Make-up flow meter, blowdown flow meter, and conductivity probe — all logged to the DCS.
  • Monthly reconciliation: make-up minus evaporation minus drift should equal blowdown within a tight tolerance.
  • Alarm on any deviation over a defined threshold, with a named responder.

Monitor the right KPI

Track make-up water per megawatt of thermal load. It is the only cooling KPI that survives changes in weather, production mix, and season. If your trend line is flat or declining, you are winning.
