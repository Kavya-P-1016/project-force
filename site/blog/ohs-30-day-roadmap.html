Occupational health & safety audits: a 30-day roadmap

A 30-day program can genuinely move a facility from "we have a manual" to a defensible, documented, inspection-ready OHS posture — without halting production. The trick is sequencing.

Week 1 · Baseline

  • Hazard identification walk-through with shift supervisors.
  • Document review: policy, SOPs, MSDS library, permit-to-work logs.
  • Incident history and near-miss log for the last 24 months.
  • First interviews — operators, contractors, security.

Week 2 · Gap analysis

Map findings against the Factories Act, BIS standards (IS 15001 or equivalent), and client-specific codes (OHSAS/ISO 45001 if relevant). Produce a weighted risk register with clear owners and deadlines — ideally five to eight "critical" items, not a 200-item wish list.

Week 3 · Rapid remediation

  • Fix the physical hazards that are cheap and fast — guards, signage, access control, emergency exits.
  • Update lockout-tagout and permit-to-work procedures; train the crew that will enforce them.
  • Run a tabletop emergency drill and document the outcomes.

Week 4 · Systems and audit-readiness

The final week is about making the program survive beyond your engagement: a named safety officer, a monthly walk-through cadence, a reviewer-signoff workflow on permits, and a KPI pack that leadership actually looks at.

What "done" looks like

At the end of 30 days, a plant has a living risk register with dated closures, an updated SOP pack, a trained crew, a drill record, and a dashboard. That is the difference between saying safety is a priority and being able to prove it.
